April 2026 Sales Recap

Q2 Month 1 -- Week 1 through Week 4 (April 1 -- April 30)

TL;DR

  • Top-of-funnel is exceptional, conversion is the bottleneck. 46 deals generated (184% of target), 48 demos completed (145% of target), 64 qualified leads -- but only $5,997 closed (12% of $50K goal). Avg deal size $500. We're winning small clinics/ATCs, not enterprise.
  • Product gaps cost us 3x what we closed. 12 of 13 lost deals had demo calls -- the product gets in front of the right people and loses at conversion. $17,358 in lost ARR driven by: integration/API needs ($5.9K), content beyond ortho/sports ($6.2K), and MedBridge switching costs ($2.2K). If product-gap losses converted, KR 1.1 would be at 38% instead of 12%. Sales will continue to help inform product roadmap to eliminate the top 3 deal-killing gaps (integrations, content breadth, custom video uploads).
  • May pivot: focus on deals we can win now + GTM Patients & Teams launch. School and enterprise deals require time + pilots and won't close this quarter. Sales shifting to close group customer profiles that we can win now.

Key Trends

Patterns from April's lost deals and sales motion

1. MedBridge is the #1 competitor and switching cost is the moat

4 deals: Duke, Pondera, Coast, Evolve -- $2,193 lost
  • Duke Health (30 staff!): "lacking integration / CEU / content to displace MedBridge"
  • Pondera: "too much hassle to switch from MedBridge, need custom video uploads"
  • Coast PT (3 locations): "staying with MedBridge due to several factors"
  • Evolve PT: chose "different software" after trial

2. Content gaps beyond ortho/sports are costing deals

3 deals: Sports MedicTT, PNWU, Duke -- $6,211 lost
  • Sports MedicTT: "lacks content tailored to geriatric population -- can't endorse for entire staff"
  • PNWU DPT: faculty said content too limited for full curriculum
  • Duke: content breadth insufficient vs MedBridge

3. Integration/API is a dealbreaker for larger orgs

3 deals: Duke, Quinn, Promotion -- $5,897 lost
  • Quinn Orthopedic (25 PTs): specifically wanted API for corporate wellness partner -- using StriveHub instead
  • Duke: "lacking integration" listed first in their reason
  • Promotion PT: multi-site, "lacking integration and content"

4. Custom video upload is a specific, repeated ask

2 deals: Pondera + implied by others
  • Pondera explicitly lost because they can't upload their own exercise videos
  • This is table stakes for MedBridge switchers

5. Leadership/DM buy-in failure -- demos go well but don't reach the signer

3 deals: PT Solutions, Amherst, PNWU -- $6,598 lost
  • PT Solutions: "leadership decision -- not bought in" despite champion interest
  • Amherst: buyer is admin, needs Dir of Sports Med on a call -- never got three-way
  • PNWU: faculty feedback killed it after champion brought it to them

April Reflection

Subjective + objective assessment

Subjective

Feeling the sense of urgency to hit 1.1 OKR as we're off-track. High desire to improve product to eliminate reasons for deals not closing. Want to help with product roadmap & marketing's strategy for prehab-pro due to significant product awareness issue.

Headspace Split

~33% strategy / 33% management / 33% execution. More time in execution due to increased calls and lead follow-up.

Ownership Split

~80% sales, 15-20% supporting marketing/product/clinic/CS via lifecycle, social, & biz knowledge

Objective

  • Key OKR (1.1) off track while leading indicators (1.2, 1.3) are doing well
  • DPT programs of 100 is very hard to measure -- can only go off of leads vs. ClickHouse. Leads are moving slower. SME not delivering on what we hoped
  • Close rate impacted by pipeline cleanup (8 stale deals closed-lost 4/30). Healthier pipeline going into May

Strategy Pivots for May

  • School & enterprise deals not likely to close this quarter & likely to require pilots. Sales needs to shift outreach/focus on groups we can win with now to help meet OKR 1.1
  • GTM patients & teams effectiveness of product features / managed patients and self-serve marketing will determine team success -- sales + team sales will need to be bundled at EOQ to evaluate performance as it relates to OKR 1.1
  • Environmental/Market factor: Federal student loan access for professional degrees -- PT no longer included starting July -- could impact DPT program budgets
