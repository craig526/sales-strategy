School Implementation Strategy

DPT & MSAT program penetration framework. 5-phase adoption model with live pipeline positioning. Updated May 18, 2026.

The Long Game

Schools are the slowest sales cycle but the highest-leverage channel. Every DPT/MSAT student who uses Prehab Professional during clinical rotations becomes a future clinician who already knows the platform. Win the program, shape student habits during training, capture the clinician when they enter practice. The immediate school ARR ($5-24K per program) is secondary to the compound downstream effect on clinic and enterprise pipeline over 3-5 years. One DPT cohort of ~40 students produces ~40 clinicians who need a HEP builder on day one of practice.

Lost Deal Analysis: PNWU DPT

From intro (3/11) to closed-lost (4/7) in 27 days. Faculty saw value in the platform. Five structural reasons it didn't convert:

  1. Wrong champion layer. Tyler Smith (Assistant Professor) had influence but no budget authority. Never reached the program director. Gene's rule: "the fastest closes share one variable -- Craig was speaking to the owner from the start."
  2. Free tools already entrenched. Faculty use HEP2Go (free, has Spanish translations) and Medbridge (already paid for via CEU anchor). Competing against inertia, not a product gap.
  3. Product-curriculum fit gap. Platform is patient-facing. Faculty need student-facing value: board prep alignment, clinical reasoning, asynchronous study content. No clean answer to "how does this make my teaching better today?"
  4. Budget cycle misalignment. No room in current fiscal year. DPT program budgets are set 6-12 months in advance.
  5. Single-champion fragility. Tyler may not teach therex next year. Entire relationship held by one rotating faculty role.
Lessons Applied
  • Target program directors from first email, use faculty intros as entry but schedule three-way calls early
  • Lead with board prep & clinical readiness outcomes, not faculty workflow
  • Use pilot-for-data framing with explicit measurement goals and time limits
  • A "no buy + strong signal" pilot is still a win for product intelligence

DPT Selling Principles

  • Program director access is the North Star. Faculty enthusiasm doesn't close deals. Every faculty champion should be treated as a path to the program director, not the final decision maker.
  • Start semester-level, not program-wide. Get one course to integrate the platform. Prove value in a contained environment. Expand from there. This is how SDSU is progressing.
  • Equip champions to sell internally. Give faculty language, data, and talking points they can bring to committees. They shouldn't be carrying our pitch -- they should be carrying their own conviction backed by evidence.
  • Three-way calls early. When a faculty champion surfaces, propose a call with the program director within the first two touchpoints. Do not let information pass through intermediaries.
  • Compete with board prep, not HEP. Medbridge and HEP2Go are entrenched for basic exercise prescription. Our wedge is student education: clinical readiness, exercise dosing expertise, board prep correlation. This is the gap nobody else fills.

MSAT Selling Principles

  • Move faster. MSAT decision structures are simpler. Get to trial quickly, set tight timelines, push for commitment. The 29-day window applies even more here -- MSAT deals that stall are usually gone.
  • Lead with athletic use cases. Return-to-play protocols, sports-specific rehab, sideline-to-clinic HEP continuity. ATs think differently than PTs -- speak their language.
  • Clinical rotation is still the wedge. Same as DPT: students using the platform on clinical rotations is where habit formation happens. The downstream clinician acquisition play is identical.

Strategic Framework

Faculty as the Keystone

Faculty buy-in makes or breaks school deals. But faculty enthusiasm alone doesn't close -- it needs to reach the program director. Every faculty champion is an internal advocate, not the final decision maker. Equip them with data and language to sell up the chain.

The Semester-Level Entry

Don't pitch full-program adoption on day one. Get one semester, one course, one faculty member to integrate the platform. Prove value in a contained environment. Expand from there. SDSU's progression validates this approach -- single cohort trial, not a program-wide mandate.

Clinical Rotation Bridge

The "aha moment" for students happens during clinical rotations when they build real HEPs for real patients. A student who sends their first patient a professional video-based exercise program remembers that experience. This is where platform habit formation happens -- and it's the strongest proof point for faculty.

Student → Clinician Pipeline

Each DPT cohort of ~40 students produces ~40 clinicians entering practice over 1-2 years. Trained on Prehab Professional, they become advocates at whatever clinic they join. At scale: 10 programs × 40 students/year = 400 new clinicians annually who already know the platform. School ARR is the seed. Clinician acquisition is the harvest.
